Svrljiški Belmuž


Jump to recipe


Belmuž is a traditional specialty of unique taste from the mountainous regions of southeastern Serbia. It's made from young sheep or cow cheese and corn flour. The key ingredient is cheese, that should be fresh and full-fat cow's or preferably sheep's cheese. Belmuž is traditionally prepared by men due to the fact that it needs to be continuously stirred as it cooks, which takes a lot of effort. It is on the list of Serbia’s intangible cultural heritage.




Prep time: 5-10 minutes Cook time: 30 minutes Serving: 6-8 servings

INGREDIENTS
  • 1kg young, unsalted sheep or cow cheese
  • 150-200g corn flour, or wheat flour
  • 1tbsp salt, optional

INSTRUCTIONS
  1. The cheese is placed in a big pot (best cooked in a cauldron) over low heat, and is stirred.
  2. As the cheese melts, flour and salt are slowly added, under constant stirring.
  3. The mixture is continuously stirred with a big wooden spoon until the fat separates along the edge of the pot and the mixture becomes compact.
  4. Belmuž is then ready to be served warm, usually as an appetizer with sour milk and various sliced vegetables.
